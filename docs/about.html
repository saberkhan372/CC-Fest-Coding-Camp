About CC Fest Coding Camp

Creative coding for all, taught through small experiments.

CC Fest is a free creative coding community for educators and learners. Coding Camp is one part of that community: a place to learn p5.js by making, noticing, changing, and sharing visual experiments.

What it is

These tools support live CC Fest sessions and independent practice. They are built for people who want code to feel more like a studio material: something you can touch, test, revise, and make your own.

What you'll find

A catalog of 135 resources: 21 concept bridges, 70 workshop tools, and 44 starter sketches, organized around the five-session arc.

Who it is for

The camp is for teachers, students, artists, designers, organizers, and curious beginners. You do not need to arrive as a programmer. You only need a willingness to try one change, see what happens, and keep going.

What happens in a session

We usually start with a shared idea, open a small interactive tool, make predictions, change values, and remix a starter sketch. The goal is not to rush through syntax. The goal is to build intuition and confidence.

How to follow along

Start at Where to start to browse by goal, or open the Sessions page to follow the five-session arc. Use a concept bridge when an idea feels fuzzy, then choose a starter sketch when you want to make something personal. Sessions happen throughout the year through CC Fest.

A note from Saber

I build these pages as teaching companions: not replacements for people learning together, but little surfaces that make a tricky idea visible. If a tool helps someone ask a better question, explain a pattern to a friend, or feel brave enough to edit the code, it is doing its job.
